GIS Analyst / Project Lead

NYC Flood Risk and Energy Infrastructure Exposure Analysis

This project mapped future flood risk scenarios across the New York City metro area and assessed exposure of critical energy infrastructure under a 2050 sea level rise moderate flood scenario. Using NYC Panel on Climate Change 90th percentile projections, I overlaid three flooding categories against transmission lines and power plants across the five boroughs to identify vulnerabilities in the city’s energy grid. The project applied a hazard-exposure framework that translates spatial risk into decision-relevant insight for urban resilience and infrastructure planning.

Wind Energy Siting and Feasibility Analysis in Nebraska

This project evaluated suitable locations for utility-scale wind energy development in Nebraska using a GIS-based multi-criteria suitability framework. By integrating wind resource data with land use, slope, environmental constraints, and proximity to transmission infrastructure, I created a reusable spatial decision-support workflow for early-stage feasibility screening. The analysis demonstrates structured site selection, layered spatial reasoning, and map production for infrastructure planning.

Undergraduate Researcher / Conference Presenter

Ukraine’s Abandoned Farmland Quantified Using Satellite Imagery

This research investigated farmland abandonment in conflict-affected areas of Ukraine following the 2021–2022 escalation of strikes near the border. Using PlanetScope SuperDove imagery, I applied NDVI-based remote sensing analysis and raster change detection to compare summer-season agricultural patterns across two years. The project connected spatial evidence to questions of food security, displacement, and agricultural production, and was presented at the AAG Summit, GEOINT Symposium, and USC Geospatial Summit.

Summer Research Associate

China and India’s High-Tech Urban Development Show Distinct Spatial Patterns

This project analyzed divergent urban development patterns in Wuhan and Bangalore between 2002 and 2020 using satellite imagery. Under Dr. Jeffrey Sellers, I created map layouts from imagery processed using Trimble Maps to examine how governance structures shape spatial growth. The analysis showed that Wuhan underwent large-scale, top-down redevelopment, replacing earlier settlement patterns, while Bangalore exhibited more incremental and organic expansion around existing village clusters.
